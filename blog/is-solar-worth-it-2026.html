1. The Game Changer: PM Surya Ghar Muft Bijli Yojana

If there is one reason 2026 is the year to switch, it is the central government's massive push for energy independence through the PM Surya Ghar Muft Bijli Yojana. Designed to light up 1 crore households, this scheme has heavily subsidized the barrier to entry.

Here is how the subsidy structure looks for residential properties today:

  • Up to 2kW System: Subsidy of ₹30,000 per kW.
  • Additional Capacity (up to 3kW): Subsidy of ₹18,000 per kW.
  • Systems larger than 3kW: Capped at a maximum direct subsidy of ₹78,000.

3. Turning Your Roof into a Revenue Generator

One of the most misunderstood concepts of modern solar is Net Metering. When you install an on-grid solar system, your home remains connected to the local power grid.

During a sunny afternoon in May, your panels will likely produce more electricity than your home is currently consuming. Instead of wasting that power, the system automatically exports it to the grid. Your electricity meter literally spins backward. The local DISCOM (electricity board) gives you credits for this exported power, which you then use at night when the sun is down. You only pay if you consume more than you produce over the entire month.
