1. What Are You Actually Paying For? (The 4 Pillars of Cost)

A solar power plant is not just a bunch of glass panels screwed to a roof. It is a highly sophisticated, grid-tied miniature power station. The total cost you are quoted by an EPC company is divided into four main categories:

A. The Solar Modules (Panels) - ~45% of Total Cost

The technology inside solar panels has evolved rapidly by 2026. To qualify for the PM Surya Ghar subsidy in Uttar Pradesh, you must use ALMM-approved (Approved List of Models and Manufacturers) Make in India panels.

We strictly utilize Monocrystalline PERC (Half-Cut) technology. Unlike older, cheaper Polycrystalline panels, Mono-PERC panels generate significantly more electricity even on cloudy or foggy winter days in Delhi NCR. While they cost slightly more upfront, their 25-year high-yield performance guarantees a faster ROI.

B. The Solar Inverter - ~20% of Total Cost

The inverter is the "brain" of your solar plant. It takes the raw DC power generated by the panels and converts it into the AC power your home appliances use. High-quality string inverters (from Tier-1 brands) ensure that power fluctuations don't damage your sensitive electronics. A cheap inverter will fail within 3 years; a premium one will last a decade or more.

C. Balance of System (BOS) - ~20% of Total Cost

This is where cheap installers cut corners to give you a "lower quote." The BOS includes everything else required to make the plant safe and functional:

  • Mounting Structures: Heavy-duty Hot Dip Galvanized (HDG) steel structures designed to withstand 150+ km/h wind speeds.
  • Cabling: UV-resistant, fire-retardant DC/AC cables. Cheap wires are the #1 cause of solar-related roof fires.
  • Safety Gear: AC/DC Distribution Boxes, Lightning Arresters (LA), and deep chemical earthing kits to protect your home from lightning strikes.

D. EPC Services & Net Metering - ~15% of Total Cost

This covers the professional engineering, labor, transportation, and the complex administrative work required to get your Bi-Directional Net Meter approved and installed by the local electricity board (PVVNL/UPPCL).

Pro Tip: The "Too Good To Be True" Quote
If a local vendor offers you a price that is 30% lower than market average, run away. They are either using rejected B-grade panels, non-ALMM imports (which voids your subsidy), or dangerously thin aluminum structures that will bend in the next monsoon storm.

2. The 3kW Solar System Cost Breakdown

The 3kW on-grid system is the undisputed king of Indian residential solar. It is the perfect size for a standard 2BHK to 3BHK home looking to eliminate a ₹3,000 to ₹4,500 monthly electricity bill.

A 3kW system produces roughly 12 to 15 units of electricity per day (depending on the season), which is enough to power standard lighting, fans, a refrigerator, a television, and 1 to 2 Inverter ACs running moderately.

Central Subsidy (PM Surya Ghar) - ₹78,000
UP State Subsidy (UPNEDA) - ₹30,000
Net Cost to You ₹72,000 - ₹87,000

ROI Timeline: Because of the massive ₹1,08,000 combined subsidy, this system pays for itself in just 2 to 3 years!

3. The 5kW Solar System Cost Breakdown

If you have a larger family, live in an independent villa, or run 3 to 4 Air Conditioners during the harsh Noida summers, a 3kW system won't be enough to drop your bill to zero. You need the heavy-lifting power of a 5kW system.

A 5kW system generates roughly 20 to 25 units per day. It requires about 400 to 500 square feet of shadow-free rooftop space.

4. The 10kW Solar System Cost Breakdown

The 10kW system is designed for luxury residential villas, multi-story family homes with shared meters, or small commercial setups like clinics, boutique hotels, and offices. This system will easily power central air conditioning, heavy water pumps, and multiple heavy appliances simultaneously.

A 10kW plant is an industrial-grade residential setup, generating an incredible 40 to 50 units per day.

10kW System Financials (2026 Estimates) Cost Details
Gross Cost (Premium Setup) ₹5,50,000 - ₹6,00,000
