1. Understanding the "Double-Engine" Subsidy in UP

Unlike some states that only rely on the central government, residents of Uttar Pradesh have a massive advantage: they benefit from two separate, stackable subsidies. When you install a solar plant, you are rewarded by both New Delhi and Lucknow.

Part A: The Central PM Surya Ghar Muft Bijli Yojana

Launched with a massive budget allocation, this national scheme aims to solarize one crore households. The subsidy is calculated strictly on the capacity (in Kilowatts) of your solar plant:

  • Up to 2kW: ₹30,000 per kW (Max ₹60,000)
  • For the 3rd kW: An additional ₹18,000
  • Systems larger than 3kW: Capped at a maximum central payout of ₹78,000.

Part B: The UPNEDA State Solar Policy

To further accelerate adoption, the Uttar Pradesh New and Renewable Energy Development Agency (UPNEDA) offers a state-level top-up subsidy exclusively for its residents.

  • Per kW Top-up: ₹15,000 per kW
  • Maximum State Cap: Capped at ₹30,000 per consumer.

3. Who is Eligible for the Uttar Pradesh Subsidy?

The rules set by the government are refreshingly straightforward, but they are strict. To claim the ₹1,08,000 subsidy in Noida or anywhere else in UP, you must meet the following criteria:

  • Residential Property Only: The installation must be on an individual household, apartment, or residential society (RWA). Commercial, industrial, and institutional properties do not qualify for this specific scheme (though they have access to Accelerated Depreciation tax benefits).
  • Active Grid Connection: You must have a valid, active electricity connection and a recent electricity bill registered in your name.
  • Rooftop Ownership: You must have legal ownership of the rooftop or explicit permission to install the array.
  • ALMM Approved (Make in India) Panels: This is the most critical rule. Cheap, imported panels will instantly disqualify your application. 4. Understanding Net Metering in Noida (PVVNL)

Many homeowners worry: "What happens if I produce more solar energy than I use during the day?"

This is where Net Metering comes in. When you install an on-grid solar system with JMD Solar, your home remains connected to the UPPCL/PVVNL power grid. During a bright, sunny afternoon, your 5kW plant will likely produce an excess of power. Instead of wasting that energy or needing expensive batteries to store it, your system automatically exports the extra electricity out to the city grid.

The standard unidirectional electricity meter on your wall is replaced by a "Bi-Directional Net Meter." It literally spins backward when you export power. The DISCOM gives you unit-for-unit credits for this exported power. At night, when the sun goes down and you run your ACs, you import power from the grid using those accumulated credits. You only pay a bill if your monthly consumption exceeds your total monthly solar production.
