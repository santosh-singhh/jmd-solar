1. The Ultimate Shield Against Rising Tariffs

Unlike residential consumers who benefit from heavily subsidized power, commercial and industrial (C&I) entities bear the brunt of India's electricity pricing. Tariffs for businesses often hover between ₹8 to ₹12 per unit (kWh), depending on the state DISCOM and peak load penalties.

Historically, these rates increase by an average of 4% to 6% annually. By installing an on-grid commercial solar plant, you achieve Grid Parity instantly. Once your system is installed, the Levelized Cost of Energy (LCOE) generated by your roof drops to approximately ₹2 to ₹3 per unit.

More importantly, you are hedging against inflation. The sun does not raise its prices. By investing in a commercial solar array with a 25-year performance warranty, you are effectively locking in your electricity rates for the next quarter of a century.

2. Accelerated Depreciation (AD) – The Ultimate Tax Benefit

While the Indian government offers direct capital subsidies for residential solar, commercial entities are excluded from those specific schemes. Instead, the government incentivizes businesses through a massive tax loophole under Section 32 of the Income Tax Act: Accelerated Depreciation (AD).

Standard business assets depreciate slowly over 10 to 15 years. However, to encourage green energy adoption, the government allows commercial solar investors to claim depreciation at a staggering 40% per year.

How the Math Works:

Let’s assume a manufacturing facility installs a 100kW solar plant for ₹50,00,000 (50 Lakhs).

  • Year 1 Depreciation: You can write off 40% of the asset value (₹20,00,000) directly from your company's taxable profits.
  • Tax Savings: If your corporate tax bracket is 25%, that ₹20 Lakh write-off translates to an immediate cash tax saving of ₹5,00,000 in the very first year.
  • Year 2 Depreciation: You claim 40% on the remaining written-down value (₹30,00,000), yielding another massive deduction.

When you combine these direct tax savings with the money saved on your monthly electricity bill, the capital cost of the system drops dramatically, accelerating your break-even point.

Pro Tip: Timing Your Installation
To claim the full 40% Accelerated Depreciation for a given financial year, your commercial solar plant must be commissioned and operational for more than 180 days in that fiscal year (typically before September 30th). If commissioned after September 30th, the depreciation allowed drops to 20% for that first year.

3. Commercial Net Metering and Gross Metering

The operational logic behind a commercial solar plant relies heavily on local state regulations regarding grid connection. Across India, there are two primary billing mechanisms available to businesses:

Net Metering

Ideal for offices, schools, and malls that operate primarily during the day. The solar energy powers your building first. Any excess power generated on weekends or holidays is exported to the grid. You are billed only for the "net" power you import from the grid minus what you export. (Note: Many states cap Net Metering for commercial setups at 500kW or 1MW).

Gross Metering

If your state does not allow Net Metering for massive industrial setups, Gross Metering is the alternative. All the power your solar plant generates is fed directly into the grid, and the DISCOM pays you a fixed Feed-In Tariff (FiT) for every unit. You continue to buy your operational electricity from the grid as usual, but the FiT revenue offsets the bill.

5. Financing: The CAPEX vs. OPEX (RESCO) Models

Not every business wants to tie up capital in an energy asset. In India, there are two primary financial models to adopt commercial solar:

Model How It Works Best For
CAPEX (Capital Expenditure) Your company pays 100% of the upfront cost and owns the plant from Day 1. You receive all the AD tax benefits and 100% of the free electricity. Cash-rich companies looking for massive tax write-offs and the fastest ROI (typically 2-3 years).
OPEX / RESCO (Renewable Energy Service Company) A third-party investor pays for, installs, and maintains the plant on your roof. You sign a Power Purchase Agreement (PPA) to buy the solar power from them at a rate 20-40% cheaper than the grid. Companies that want zero upfront investment, zero maintenance liability, and instant monthly savings.
